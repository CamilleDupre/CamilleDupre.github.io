Camille Dupré

Camille.dupre@berger-levrault.com

I am a CIFRE PhD student in the ILDA research team and Berger-Levrault company. I am working in Human-Computer Interaction and more particularly in Augmented Reality (AR) and immersive experiences. My research focuses on developing innovative AR input techniques to better understand of how AR can be effectively integrated into various fields, such as industry.

My thesis supervisors are Emmanuel Pietriga and Caroline Appert

Experience

PHD in Human Computer interaction

Berger-Levrault / INRIA

In my thesis, I explore pad-based interaction in augmented reality (AR), focusing on how passive surfaces, such as tables, walls, or the human body, can serve as intuitive support for interaction with virtual content. This approach leverages the familiarity and precision of touch interactions to control and interact with AR elements. This research aims to develop new interaction techniques that make AR experiences more accessible, user-friendly, and effective for various applications such as industrial usage.

During this internship, I worked on augmenting tangible controllers in augmented reality (AR). The goal was to enhance the way users interact with digital content through physical objects.
